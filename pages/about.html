About the T20 World Cup Journey

How Has the Journey of the T20 World Cup Been Over the Years?

The T20 World Cup, first played in 2007, is an international cricket championship, usually held once every two years. Initially, it began as a 12-team competition in 2007. It was expanded to 16 teams from the 2014 edition and further extended to 20 teams for the 2024 edition, with teams divided into four groups.

India’s Journey Over the Years

India clinched the inaugural T20 World Cup in South Africa in 2007, defeating Pakistan in the final under the leadership of MS Dhoni. In 2014, India finished as the runner-up, losing to Sri Lanka in the final.

Why in the News?

The Indian cricket team secured the ICC 2024 Men’s T20 World Cup title with a narrow victory over South Africa in Barbados on June 29, 2024. This win marked India's second T20 World Cup title and its first in 17 years.

Winners of Multiple T20 World Cups

  • India (2007 & 2024)
  • England (2010 & 2022)
  • West Indies (2012 & 2016)

With their victory in 2022, England became the first team to simultaneously hold both men’s World Cups - the 2019 ODI World Cup and the 2022 T20 World Cup.

Key Highlights of the 2024 T20 Final

Individual Feats of the 2024 Cup

  • Virat Kohli is the leading run-getter in T20 World Cup history with 1292 runs in 35 matches since his debut in 2012.
  • Shakib Al Hasan of Bangladesh is the top wicket-taker in T20 World Cup history, with 50 wickets in 43 matches between 2007 and 2024.
  • Player of the Match: Virat Kohli
  • Player of the Tournament: Jasprit Bumrah
  • Most Wickets in a T20 World Cup Edition:
    1. 17 - Arshdeep Singh (IND, 2024)
    2. 17 - Fazalhaq Farooqi (AFG, 2024)

Lowest Economy Rate in a T20 World Cup Edition

  • 4.17 - Jasprit Bumrah (2024)

Additional Highlights

  • It was only the 3rd instance where a team won the T20 World Cup final while defending the target, following India in 2007 and West Indies in 2012.
  • India became the first team to win a T20 World Cup while remaining unbeaten throughout the tournament.
  • Post World Cup victory, three Indian players - Virat Kohli, Rohit Sharma, and Ravindra Jadeja - announced their retirement from T20 internationals.
  • The BCCI announced a prize money of Rs 125 crore for Team India following their T20 World Cup win.
